A Different Kind of Workforce

The crew that builds something.

The typical QSR store runs on a pool of 20 workers — many of whom, at the onset of their employment, were never planning to stay. They're between jobs, filling time, taking the shift while looking for something else. That is not a character flaw. That is a rational response to a work experience that was never designed to be worth staying for.

OllieBoard changes what the job offers. When day one is real, when harder work is immediately reflected in pay, when there's a portable record worth building — the store starts attracting a different kind of worker. One who intends to stay.

20→15
A crew of 20 that turns over constantly is replaced, over time, by a stable crew of 15 — better trained, better paid, lower total cost.

Fewer Replacement Hires

When workers stay longer, you run fewer replacement cycles. The budget that was paying to onboard the same position twice a year now pays one person better.

Workers who stay get good. They cover more zones. Two partially trained people doing one job becomes one capable person doing it well. The headcount need drops organically.

Not "stays the same." Decreases. The savings are not from cutting — they are from not replacing. Each dollar goes further when it goes to someone who stayed, got good, and earns what they actually gave.

Why This Exists

Walt Disney World, Orlando. One of 13.

"From 2000 to 2010, my wage went up one dollar. Inflation went up 28%. I worked 13 jobs in 10 years — not because I was lazy. Because no system ever told me the truth."

Rahkeem Morris worked 13 hourly jobs in a decade — restaurants, retail, facilities. He was the revolving door. Then he went through Traditions at Disney World. For the first time, someone gave him a reason to stay. He never forgot what that felt like.

He went on to connect over 10 million workers to employment. Then he learned that reaching workers from the outside wasn't enough. The system had to change from inside the store. OllieBoard is built from inside real franchise locations in Massachusetts.

The founding belief: every hourly worker deserves to go to work and have it be stress free. To know what to do. To earn what they actually gave. To have a first day worth staying for.

Rahkeem Morris · Founder & CEO, OllieBoard · Commissioner for the Future of Work, Commonwealth of Massachusetts
